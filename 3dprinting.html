3D Printing

I learned the basics of 3D printing, the different types of 3D printers (SLS, SLA, FDM),

and how to use slicing software to prepare 3D models for printing.

Week 6 - Exercise 11 & 12

In these two exercises, I was required to create a 3D model of a chess piece and print

it out. I decided to make the Rook.

After creating the model I exported it into the Ultimaker Cura slicer software to

prepare it for printing.

Here is a picture of the piece after printing out and removing supports.

Assignment 1

In this assignment I was tasked to design and 3D print a phone stand. I wanted my design

to be adjustable so that the phone could rest a different angles. I also wanted

it to be foldable and compact so that it is easier to carry around.

So I tried my best to incorporate these features into my design.

The 3D models below show the folded and unfolded states of the main body of the stand.

I designed some pegs to help fit and and attach the phone rest components to the main body, shown below.

Each of these pieces were printed separately to avoid using too many supports.

Below are some photos of the assembled final product.

The cutouts on the base allow the user to adjust the angle of the stand.
