Programming & Arduino

For this section, I learned to use TinkerCAD to wire and programme digital circuits and simulate their functions.

Below are some exercise I've done on TinkerCAD.

Week 11 - Exercise 13

As an introduction to TinkerCAD, I learned to wire a simple circuit on an Arduino Uno

I then programed it to blink once every other second.

Week 11 - Exercise 14

In this exercise I wired up a 7 segment display to some toggle switches on a breadboard.

The input of the switches changes the digits on the display.

week 11 - Exercise 15

In this exercise I used an Arduino UNO to program a 7 segment display to

continuously count from 0 to 9.

Week 11 - Exercise 16

Here I wired up an LED to an Arduino UNO and programed it to blink continuously again,

but now with the number of blinks being counted and displayed in the serial monitor.

Input Devices

In this section I learned how to wire and program common input devices.

Week 12 - Exercise 17

Here the input is a matrix keypad, the Arduino was programmed to read the inputs

and print it in the serial monitor.

Week 12 - Exercise 18

The input here is a photoresistor, the circuit is programmed to turn on the LED when

the photoresistor detects a certain threshold of light.

Week 12 - Exercise 19

Here the input is an ultrasonic sensor to detect nearby objects. It was programmed

to trigger an alarm response if it detect something within a certain distance to it.

Week 12 - Exercise 20

Here a PIR Motion Sensor detects movement and displays a count of the number of times

motion was detected in the serial monitor.

Week 12 - Exercise 21

Here the circuit uses temperature sensor as input. Depending on the temperature reading, a different

number of LEDs will light up. The higher the temperature, the more LEds light up.

Week 12 - Exercise 22

Here a potentiometer is used to control the brightness of an LED. The higher the

input value from the potentiometer, the brighter the LED.

Output Devices

Week 12 - Exercise 23

In this exercise, an RGB LED is used as an output. The circuit was programmed to randomly blink

red, green or blue continuously.

Week 12 - Exercise 24

In this exercise, two Neopixels were linked together and programmed to flash different

colours continuously.

Week 12 - Exercise 25

Here a DC motor is used as output. The motor is wired to a transistor to act as a tap to control

the electric signals and potentiometer to adjust the RPM.

Week 12 - Exercise 26

In this exercise, a stepper motor is wired up and programmed to have different speeds and

steps per revolution values.

Week 12 - Exercise 27

Here a servo motor is programmed to rotate bit by bit at a certain angle.

The angle of rotation is printed in the serial monitor..

Week 12 - Exercise 28

Here a servo motor is programmed to rotate continuously with a potentiometer to control the speed

direction of rotation.

Week 12 - Exercise 30

Here a piezo is programmed to emit sound in certain pattern continuously.
