Final Project

This page is where I document my design process for the final project of my elective.

1.Initial Concept

The idea I came up with was to combine an automatic rubbish bin with a plastic bag sealer.

I thought it could be somewhat useful if I could managed to make it work.

The basic way I envisioned it was a rubbish bin that has a plastic sealer that can be mounted onto it, with an ultrasonic sensor to tirgger

the lid opening mechanism. In initial concept sketches, I planned to to create a half-cylindrical body out of laser cut plywood, using a living.

hinge. But after some consideration i figured that such a design would make the walls very fragile, and would require some internal supporting

structures to hold its shape. I also had to reconsider the original linkage mechanism for the lid as it was unneccessarily complicated

In the end, I decided to go with a cuboid shape, with only the front corners featuring living hinges for a more aesthetically pleasing shape.

For the lid mechanism, I just kept it simple and decided to try using a servo motor to open the lid directly.

2. 3D-Modelling

After the design considerations, I began 3D modelling the main body in Fusion 360. First I created the front wall with the living hinge, as well as

cutouts for the plastic sealer and wiring. Then I created the backwall and some internal platforms for the servo motor and ciruit cover.

I then created the external features such as the hinge for the lid and the cover for the ultrasonic sensor.

For the plastic sealer, I had to refer to online recources to see how I could replicate the mechanism.

I then recreated the mechanism in Fusion but scaled everything down so that it could fit into a smaller housing and work with a smaller roll of tape.

Here's the model of everything put together.

3. Programming

I wired up, and programmed the circuit in TinkerCAD. The circuit consists of an ultrasonic sensor and a servo motor wired to a breadboard,

with an Arduino UNO as a microcontroller.

Here is the code used. It basically programmes the servo to rotate 90 degrees whenever an object is detected by the sensor within 30cm.

4. Laser cutting

I arranged my parts into a flat pattern and exported the vector 2D profile to laser cut.

All of the main body as well as the sealer cover was made using laser cut 3mm plywood

5. 3D Printing

I used the Ultimaker Cura slicer to prepare my 3D printed parts. I tried to configure the print settings to have a faster print time whenever possible

as I had many parts to print.

After printing out the parts for the sealer, I assembled them together and put in the springs and blade to make the mechanism work.

Here are some of my other 3D printed parts.

6. Assembly

Once the parts have all been fabricated, I started building the project. The main body was mainly put together by finger joints, with some glue to reinforce

parts that don't fit well due wood warping. I also used nuts and bolts to fasten the hinge to the lid and back wall. For the rest of the loose componets,

I used hot glue to fix them to the body.

7. Challenges

There were multiple problems that came up during the design process that needed to be solved either through redesigning or modifying some parts

using crude DIY methods.

1. The initial design for the hinge was too thin, leading to the shaft of the hinge to be fragile and break during assembly. To fix this, I

redesigned the hinge so that its thicker at the section with the mechanism to make it stronger.

2. The slits in the living hinge were too long, causing it to fray at the edges and lack structure.

To fix this i redesigned the living hinge to have double the number of slits along

the height of the wall, and made each slit shorter.

3. The ultrasonic sensor was giving a lot of false positives and would trigger the servo motor even when there no object

in range. To fix this, i changed the code so that intead of using the sensor's readings to activate the motor directly,

the readings are averaged out and compared against a threshold value. The motor is only activated when the average

readings is falls below the threshold, which allow the circuit to be more consistent with less false positives.

4. Another issue was that the sound of the lid closing was very loud and disturbing. To fix this, I hot glued some styrofoam

padding around the rim of the bin to cushion the lid when it closes and reduce the noise.
